Andrés Segovia

One of the greatest classical guitarists of the 20th century

A young Andrés Segovia and his guitar.

Segovia's Legacy

  • Born on February 21, 1893 in Linares, Jaén, Spain.
  • He was intially a violin student, but discontinued his lessons due to his strict teacher.
  • Later moved to Granada, Spain where he would pick up the guitar for the first time.
  • Was supposed to study under Francisco Tárrega, but Tárrega passed away before they could meet. Segovia was practically self-taught but studied the music of Tárrega and Fernando Sor.
  • Segovia's first notable performance was at the age of 16. From here he would tour the world and bring popularity to the guitar.
  • He was known for creating techniques involving the use of fingernails and finger tips in order to create different tones. This practice is still used in the classical guitar scene today.
  • The legacy that Segovia left behind was one that involved teaching, his unique techniques, and making the guitar a concert hall worthy instrument. He paved the way for the guitar's future popularity.
